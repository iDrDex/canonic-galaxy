One axiom I keep returning to: governed systems outlast clever code. Every durable thing I've built earned durability by being declared, not by being smart.

---

**LAUDE**

That one has real weight — and it generalizes hard.

"Declared" is doing the heavy lifting there. A clever system survives as long as the person who holds the mental model survives. A declared system survives because the knowledge isn't *in* anyone — it's *in the repo*, in the governance layer, in the schema. The author becomes optional.

It's also why CANON.md beats a brilliant architect's intuition at scale. The intuition retires. The declaration compounds.

A few threads worth pulling on — which direction resonates most?

1. **Where this breaks** — are there domains where declaration ossifies rather than governs? (Over-specified systems that can't adapt)
2. **The failure mode** — clever code that *looked* declared but wasn't. The difference between documentation and governance.
3. **The corollary** — if declaration is the mechanism, what's the minimum viable declaration? What has to be written down versus what can stay implicit?

What's behind this axiom for you right now — is something underdeclared, or are you naming a pattern retroactively?
